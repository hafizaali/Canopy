What do we do?


Our aims are many, but mostly to create a sense of community in the gardening world and to plant plants to benefit the next generation Our motto: "Plants are friends, not pests"
-We support the projects of our members by developing confidence, self-esteem through exciting horicultural practices.
-We connect our members by involving them in our horticultural projects through out London.
-We offer further support and training, as well as paid and voluntary work in horticulture.
-We want to create a sense of community. We encourage team work whenever we can. It takes a village to grow a forest.
-We want to create and maintain seasonal agricultural through flower and vegetable gardens. We track the journey of our plants from seedlings to full grown friends.

What we do:
-We meet weekly at the London Gardening House to maintain the garden area and the grounds.
-We plan projects regularly and meetings in order to identify job roles and what more we can do in our community.
-Together we meet regularly to visit garden centre, parks and botantical gardens to gather ideas and information. We also have regular excursions.

Opportunities:
-Opportunities to learn on communal ground maintainence and gardening techniques.
-Not only will you be able to master your own private gardens, but Canopy is also connected with gardening job providers and offers CV writing workshops and career services.
-You will also learn how to maintain the communal grounds of the centre which will give you experience of a variety of gardening techniques.
-Examples of jobs some of our members have gone on to do or are availabe include:
-Gardeners in local park, park rangers on nature reserves, and botanical garden vistor guides.


What we expect:
Regular attendence
Team work
Health and Saftey rule adherence.
Following instructions of team leaders
Commited to Canopy's core value that: Plants can really change the world.
